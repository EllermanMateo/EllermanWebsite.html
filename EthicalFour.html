As technology advances every day, there is the concern of automation machines taking over the workplace, consequently ending in increasing rates of unemployment according to a YouTube video "Humans not need apply"- CGP Grey. One reason is that programmers are enabling algorithms in machines that permit them to think and learn by independently through experience. On the other hand, an article by The Economist "Automation and Anxiety" eases the worries of workers by saying that even though machines might take up some jobs, the bigger effect would be that more jobs would be created. Another article by McKinsey&&Company "The digital future of work: What will automation change?" includes interviews by professors from New York University as well as other high-ranking CEOs of other companies. The main points issued were that it is inevitable that jobs will be replaced by robots, by that the future should be looked with optimism which is essential for growing the economy as well as for human progress. After all these articles, one great dilemma is that there is the potential for a growing inequality gap between the low-skilled and the highly-skilled individuals. Moreover, as lower-skilled jobs are being substituted, people who are unable to pursue a higher education for uncontested jobs are in danger of unemployment. In addition, there are approximately 11 million immigrants illegally in U.S.A. (Washington Post) who generally incapable to attend college or finish high school as many are illiterate or are fearful of being exposed by federal agencies and sent to their country of origin. However, I personally feel that despite growing gaps, the benefits far outweigh the repercussions and is necessary for the country to be able to meet the demands of the growing population as there is the danger of overpopulation in the world. Machines can do things that no human could physically and mentally accomplish with greater accuracy and precision. There is not fear for artificial intelligence takeover of the world as depicted in movies like Terminator or Ready Player One. Nevertheless, there is the uncertainty that machines can be used in wrongful intentions that could harm lives such as someone hacking a self-driving car and causing it to malfunction. Also, a concern is that humans can become too reliant on machines that perform very manageable tasks that can make people passive and in the event of a global power failure, could cripple the entire human race. Still, I am learning Computer Science in the hopes of understanding the complexity of artificial intelligence and plan on contributing to the rise of automation.
